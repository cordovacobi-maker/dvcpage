About Davao Vision Colleges

Davao Vision Colleges, Inc. is a school that doesnt just teach academic lessons but also instill servant-leader values and godly characteristics - thanks to its weekly chapel service. Established in 2007, it upholds the mission of delivering quality education and service excellence. The college continues to nurture future leaders and innovators, staying true to its vision of valuing life through excellence in education.

Davao Vision Colleges Timeline History

2007

Rev. Dr. Kim Won Sun, Senior Pastor and Chairman of Yeomchang Presbyterian Church in Seoul, envisioned creating a non-sectarian school to train students as knowledgeable, value-driven leaders and missionaries. With the help of Rev. David Sang in Davao City, three hectares of land were purchased for the school site. Key contributors to founding Davao Vision Colleges (DVC) included Rev. Iluminado D. Macahig Jr., Dr. Cheol Woo Lee, and other dedicated Korean missionaries.

2008

Rev. Kim In Han, a Korean missionary from Seoul, helped manage the college finances and oversee construction. During the CHED recognition process, Dr. Fidel D. Japos of DepEd Regional Office XI, Dr. Rosita J. Bustamante of CHED Regional Office XI, and Rev. Abdon C. Canonoy Jr., a former college dean, provided valuable assistance in preparing and submitting the required documents. A College Board officially established in April 2008. The SEC registration was formally approved on April 30, 2008 and in February 2009. CHED granted permits for the following degree programs: Bachelor of Arts in Theology, Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Bachelor of Elementary Education, Bachelor of Secondary Education and Bachelor of Science in Information Technology.

2010

A new missionary couple, Engr. Yoom Bum Eul and Mrs. Kim Won Suk took over the financial administration and missionary responsibilities of the college. They served faithfully until 2023. Under their leadership, the partnership between DVC and Yeochang Church deepened, and the college experienced remarkable growth, from just 70 students in its early years to over 800 students.

2023

DVC underwent a significant administrative transition as Dr. Youjin Chung succeeded Mr. Yoon Bum Eul, who had led the institution faithfully for 13 years. Under Dr. Chung's leadership, the college embraced a renewed vision for its mission and future development.

2024

By the Academic Year 2024-2025, Davao Vision Colleges reached a new milestone, with an enrollment of 962 students and a dedicated team of 30 faculty and staff.

Admissions

Admission Requirements

  • Completed Application Form
  • Form 138 (Report Card)
  • Certificate of Good Moral Character
  • 2 pcs. 2x2 ID Picture (white background)
  • Birth Certificate (PSA Copy)
  • Entrance Examination Result

Offered Courses

College Programs

  • Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in Mathematics
  • Bachelor of Elementary Education
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Technology
  • Bachelor of Arts in Theology

Senior High School Programs

  • STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics)
  • HUMSS (Humanities and Social Sciences)
  • GAS (General Academic Strand)
  • TVL-ICT (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood ICT)
  • ABM (Accountancy, Business and Management)

Steps to Apply

  1. Visit the DVC Admission Office or apply online.
  2. Submit all required documents.
  3. Take the entrance exam and interview.
  4. Wait for admission confirmation.
  5. Pay enrollment fees and attend orientation.
